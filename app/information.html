What is a mechanical keyboard?

A mechanical keyboard is a keyboard that features mechanical switches, which are
switches that operate using a mechanical toggle as opposed to optically registering
key presses.

Why mechanical keyboards?

A mechanical keyboard typically chosen over an optical keyboard in that they have
desirable physical aspects over optical. Namely, there is a present force needed to
move the key down in order to register a key press, as opposed to the automatic method
utilized by optical. Further, there is often an auditory clicking, which many users
find satisfying.

What aspects of mechanical keyboards are important?

Generally, there are two main deciding characteristics users look for in mechanical keys:
actuation strength required and level of noise (often described as "clickiness".
Beyond this, there is actuation length (how far you have to press a key) and minor,
less quantifiable issues that boil down to minute personal preference.
