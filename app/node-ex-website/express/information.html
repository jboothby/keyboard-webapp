What is a Mechanical Keyboard?

Mechanical Keyboard

A mechanical keyboard is a keyboard that features mechanical switches, which are switches that operate using a mechanical toggle as opposed to optically registering key presses. They are often desirable over membrane keyboards because they require a present force to depress the key and offer a tactile or auditory response. This response often improves keystroke accuracy.

Membrane Keyboard

Most computer and laptop keyboards fall into the membrane keyboard category. Under the key caps are small rubber domes. When the key is pressed, the rubber dome collapses and presses on a plastic sheet with small electrical connections. These connections then press against a base sheet with matching connections. When the two make contact, a key press is registered. This results in a squishy feeling with no tactile response.

What aspects of mechanical keyboards are important?

Generally, there are two main deciding characteristics users look for in mechanical keys: actuation strength required and level of noise (often described as "clickiness". Beyond this, there is actuation length (how far you have to press a key) and minor, less quantifiable issues that boil down to minute personal preference.
